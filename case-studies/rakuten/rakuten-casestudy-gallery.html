Context

🔴 The Challenge

Global telecom operators managed carrier onboarding, eSIM provisioning, bilateral pricing agreements and SMS routing through fragmented manual workflows and disconnected legacy systems. Every task — from negotiating carrier agreements to configuring routing rules — required excessive effort and specialist knowledge, slowing operations across regions.

🟢 The Approach

Design a unified suite of enterprise platforms, each purpose-built for a specific telecom domain — simplifying complex workflows into intuitive, data-dense interfaces that operations, product and carrier management teams could use confidently across Japan, Singapore, Dubai and India. Each platform was designed to reduce configuration errors, improve visibility, and scale globally.

Key Learnings

🌏
Global ≠ GenericDifferent regions had different workflows and compliance expectations. Designing for Japan and Dubai in parallel required constant localisation discussions with PMs and regional leads.
📊
Density is a FeatureEnterprise telecom users need data-rich interfaces. I learned to embrace density — the goal was scannable, not sparse. Every column and filter earned its place through user research.
⚙️
Understand the BackendReading the AWS architecture diagram directly with engineers helped me design smarter — I understood why certain fields existed and what latency constraints affected real-time views.
🔗
Consistency Across ProductsManaging UX cohesion across four platforms required a shared component library. Establishing this early saved weeks of rework and improved handoff quality with engineering.
