User Research Process

I What is your process for conducting user research?

  1. Define Objectives: I collaborate with stakeholders to understand the project goals and identify specific research questions.
  2. Choose Research Methods: Depending on the objectives, I select appropriate methods such as user interviews, surveys, usability testing, or contextual inquiries.
  3. Recruit Participants: I ensure the participant pool represents our target audience by creating detailed user personas and screening candidates.
  4. Collect and Analyze Data: During the research, I collect both qualitative and quantitative data and analyze it using thematic analysis, affinity mapping, or tools like Dovetail.
  5. Synthesize Insights: Finally, I share insights with the team through presentations, journey maps, or reports, ensuring they align with business goals and inform design decisions.

II Can you give an example of a user research project you conducted?

  1. Objective: My goal was to identify pain points in the existing checkout flow.
  2. Methods: I conducted usability testing with 10 participants representing our primary user base and analyzed session recordings and heatmaps from the existing flow.
  3. Findings: Research revealed confusion around mandatory fields, lack of clarity in error messages, and an overwhelming number of steps.
  4. Actionable Changes: Based on these findings, we streamlined the process into three steps, added inline error validation, and provided a summary page before payment.
  5. Results: Post-launch, we saw a 20% increase in checkout completion rates and a 15% reduction in customer support queries.

III How do you ensure user research findings are actionable for the team?

  1. Collaborate Early: I involve stakeholders and team members from the beginning to align research objectives with project goals.
  2. Clear Documentation: I present findings using visual formats like personas, journey maps, and prioritized lists of insights.
  3. Recommendations: I pair insights with concrete recommendations, such as specific design changes or usability improvements.
  4. Workshops: I host workshops or brainstorming sessions to help the team digest insights and co-create solutions.
  5. Follow Up: I stay engaged throughout the design process, ensuring insights are implemented effectively and validated through testing.

IV How do you recruit participants for user research?

  1. Internal Tools: If the product has an existing user base, I collaborate with marketing or customer support teams to reach out to users via emails or in-app notifications.
  2. External Sources: For new audiences, I use recruitment platforms like UserTesting or Respondent.io.
  3. Screening: I create a screener questionnaire to ensure participants match the criteria and weed out unqualified candidates.
  4. Incentives: I offer appropriate incentives, such as gift cards or discounts, to encourage participation.

V How do you handle conflicting feedback from users?

  1. Identifying Patterns: I look for recurring themes or patterns across moltiple data points.
  2. Contextualizing Feedback: I consider the user’s context, persona, and specific use case to understand why their feedback might differ.
  3. Prioritizing by Goals: I align the feedback with business goals and user needs to determine what’s most critical.
  4. Validating Through Testing: If necessary, I conduct A/B testing or additional research to validate conflicting insights.

VI What tools do you use for user research?

  1. Recruitment: UserTesting, Respondent.io, or internal customer databases.
  2. Data Collection: Zoom for remote interviews, Optimal Workshop for card sorting, and Hotjar for behavior analysis.
  3. Collaboration: Miro or FigJam for mapping user journeys and affinity diagrams.
  4. Analysis and Reporting: Excel for quantitative data and Dovetail for qualitative analysis.

VII How do you measure the success of user research?

  1. Actionable Insights: Whether the research findings led to specific, implementable design changes.
  2. Improved Metrics: Post-implementation metrics like increased task completion rates, reduced error rates, or higher customer satisfaction.
  3. Team Feedback: Whether stakeholders found the insights clear and helpfol.
  4. User Feedback: Conducting follow-up usability tests or surveys to validate the effectiveness of the implemented changes.
